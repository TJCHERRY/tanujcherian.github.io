AUM:THE GAME

Try The Game Here

AUM - The Game is a Free to Play , Real Time Multi-Player Battle Arena Game inspired by Indian Mythological Lore and the Wisdom contained in ancient scriptures as the Core philosophy for story telling while simultaneously creating a game that integrates many elements of our culture that have not been explored enough into an engaging experience for the Gamer.

Developed by Brahman Studios. I joined the team back in Ocober 2021 as the sole gameplay programmer to help develop their existing prototype all the way into its Open Beta. This resulted in the development of many gameplay systems, mechanics that had to be synced with our custom architected back-end, and writing the user experience, carefully crafted by our design team, building BOTS, and executing all this with optimization for mobile as our prime focus.

The primary dev environment also known as a game engine used here is the widely popular Unity3D. Its runtime environment is .NET and C# is primarily used to communicate with the engine's core components via Unity's proprietery API.

Gameplay Mechanics

  • Since our backend didn't support the in-engine physics system, we relied on the character controller component for player movement and used a player prediction system to mitigate network lag.
  • The game has 5 different weapon yielding Fighters. Each player has the ability to choose out of 5 types of elementals and out of 3 God types before entering Battle, doing so will changing each fighter's play style and stats in-game. Thus, Building Systems to scale and modify was the goal from the get go.
  • Each of the player controllers were controlled by state machines that buffered player input and blocked certain input through enum based Input flagging assigned to each and every state, This helped us accomodate n number of player states while managing them with ease.
  • Emphasis on vector math was a non-negotiable.

Bots

In Early June of 2023, I was tasked with creating bots, so our existing player base had a backup of players available incase they fell-short. We used a straight forward approach of having an overlay bot controller that were constantly searching for matches being made and would join if matches we're available. These bots were end to end functional and also were made to follow the rules of the game. So each bot was nothing but a headless unity windows instance running on our servers, or any availble pc (while testing). I decided to create a custom yet very simple behavior tree, that was tasked to follow targets, attack, defend and also cast ultimate abilites at opportune moments.

MY EXPERIENCE

The two years I spent on this project was nothing short of a challenge every step of the way.

  • We were a team of 5 for the most part and were limited on budgets and thus resources. So finding creative and logical solutions was a daily affair.
  • Being resourceful and learning how to churn the best output out of what we have available was a practice we got really skilled at.
  • Managing the frontend of a MOBA, that in most other MOBA's would take a much larger team was a highly rewarding experince as it pushed me to the deep-end from the get go.
  • Gave me the confidence to scale and manage a larger code-base.
  • All this was possible due to the fact that I was part of an incredibly Resilient team at Brahman, that was known to punch through obstacles.
